# Fundraising Deck Guide

## Executive Summary

This guide provides a comprehensive framework for creating a compelling pitch deck and managing the fundraising process for a prediction market platform. Based on successful raises by Polymarket ($2B at $9B valuation), Kalshi ($1B at $11B valuation), and dYdX ($87M total), this document covers pitch structure, investor targeting, and due diligence preparation.

---

## 1. Pitch Deck Structure (12-15 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title/Cover

**Purpose:** First impression, establish credibility

**Content:**
- Company name and logo
- Tagline (one sentence value proposition)
- Your name and title
- Date and round information

**Example:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                             │
│           [COMPANY LOGO]                    │
│                                             │
│     "The Future of Prediction Markets"      │
│                                             │
│        Series A - $15M Round                │
│                                             │
│        [Founder Name], CEO                  │
│              January 2026                   │
│                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Slide 2: Problem

**Purpose:** Create urgency and relatability

**Key Messages:**
1. Traditional prediction methods are broken
2. Information markets are fragmented and inaccessible
3. Current platforms have significant limitations

**Content Framework:**
```
Problem Statement:

1. Information Inefficiency
   - $10T+ in decisions made with poor probability data
   - Traditional polls/forecasts are unreliable
   - Markets have better predictive accuracy

2. Market Accessibility
   - Existing platforms are US-restricted or crypto-complex
   - High fees (up to 15% on some platforms)
   - Poor UX drives away mainstream users

3. Trust & Transparency
   - Centralized platforms can manipulate odds
   - Settlement disputes are common
   - No verifiable audit trail

Quote: "Prediction markets consistently outperform polls and
expert forecasts, yet remain inaccessible to most users."
```

### Slide 3: Solution

**Purpose:** Present your unique approach

**Content:**
```
Our Solution: [Platform Name]

Core Innovation:
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Decentralized  │  Zero Fees  │  Global Access   │
│  Settlement     │  on Trading │  Multi-platform  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Key Differentiators:
1. Blockchain-based settlement (trustless, transparent)
2. Zero trading fees (0.01% max for US regulated)
3. Intuitive UX (Web2-like experience, Web3 security)
4. Deep liquidity through automated market makers
5. Comprehensive market coverage (politics, sports, crypto)

Technology Stack:
- Polygon/Base for low-cost transactions
- USDC/USDT for stable value
- Gnosis Safe for treasury security
```

## 2. Narrative Crafting Tips

### 2.1 Story Arc Framework

**The Hero's Journey for Startups:**
1. **The World as It Is** (Problem) - Prediction markets are broken/inaccessible
2. **The Call to Adventure** (Opportunity) - Massive market, timing is now
3. **Crossing the Threshold** (Solution) - Your unique approach
4. **Tests and Allies** (Traction) - What you've proven
5. **The Ordeal** (Competition) - Why you'll win
6. **The Reward** (Business Model) - How value is captured
7. **The Road Back** (Ask) - What you need
8. **Return with Elixir** (Vision) - The better future

### 2.2 Messaging Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Lead with the "why" - why this matters
- Use concrete numbers over vague claims
- Show rather than tell (demos, metrics)
- Address the elephant in the room (regulation)
- Connect to larger trends (crypto adoption, sports betting legalization)

**Don't:**
- Overstate market size without justification
- Ignore competition or say "we have no competitors"
- Use jargon without explanation
- Make claims you can't back up
- Underestimate regulatory complexity

### 2.3 Handling Tough Questions

**"What about regulation?"**
```
"Regulation is a feature, not a bug. The 2024 DC Circuit ruling
allowing Kalshi to offer election contracts shows the direction
of travel. We're taking a compliance-first approach with [specific
licenses/applications]. Our legal team includes [notable names]
who have navigated this before."
```

**"What about Polymarket?"**
```
"Polymarket validated the market and proved demand. But they're
primarily crypto-native and non-US focused. We're building for
[specific segment] with [specific differentiators]. The market
is large enough for multiple winners."
```

**"Why won't a big player just copy you?"**
```
"Great question. Our moats include: 1) Network effects - liquidity
attracts liquidity; 2) Regulatory first-mover advantage; 3)
Technology that took [X] months to build; 4) Team expertise that's
hard to replicate. Plus, incumbents have proven slow to enter
crypto-native markets."
```

## 4. Investor FAQ with Answers

### 4.1 Business Model Questions

**Q: How do you make money if trading is free?**
A: "While we offer zero or minimal trading fees to maximize volume, we monetize through: 1) Premium subscriptions for advanced features ($29-499/month), 2) API access and data licensing, 3) B2B/white-label partnerships, and 4) Market creation fees. This mirrors successful models in crypto (Robinhood, exchanges) where free trading drives volume that monetizes elsewhere."

**Q: What's your path to profitability?**
A: "We project break-even at approximately Month 11 with $93M in monthly trading volume. Our unit economics show a 5-7x LTV:CAC ratio with 6-9 month payback periods. The business model scales efficiently - marginal cost per additional trade is near zero."

**Q: How does your take rate compare to competitors?**
A: "Our effective take rate of 0.3-0.5% positions us between Polymarket (0-0.01%) and Kalshi (~1.2%). This balances competitiveness with sustainable unit economics. We can adjust based on market conditions."

### 4.2 Market & Competition

**Q: How big is the prediction market opportunity really?**
A: "The TAM depends on how you define it. Narrowly, existing prediction markets are ~$10B. Broadly, we're competing with: 1) Sports betting ($100B+ global), 2) Trading/speculation broadly, 3) Information/research budgets. Polymarket did $9B in volume in 2024 alone, up from $73M in 2023 - proving rapid growth potential."

**Q: What if Polymarket dominates?**
A: "Polymarket has done incredible work validating the market. However: 1) They're primarily crypto-native (complex onboarding), 2) They're restricted in the US, 3) The market is large enough for multiple winners. We're targeting [specific segment] where we have advantages."

**Q: What's your regulatory strategy?**
A: "We're taking a multi-pronged approach: 1) [Specific jurisdiction] licensing application in progress, 2) Compliance-first architecture (KYC/AML built in), 3) Legal team includes [notable advisors], 4) Monitoring regulatory developments actively. The 2024 Kalshi ruling was a positive signal for the industry."

### 4.3 Technology & Product

**Q: What's your technology moat?**
A: "Our core moat is: 1) Proprietary order-matching algorithm optimized for prediction markets, 2) [X] months of engineering in platform infrastructure, 3) Smart contracts audited by [top firm], 4) Network effects - liquidity attracts more liquidity."

**Q: How do you handle market resolution?**
A: "We use a hybrid oracle system: 1) Primary resolution through trusted data sources (AP, official results), 2) Decentralized dispute mechanism for edge cases, 3) Clear resolution criteria published pre-market. Smart contract audit by [firm] validated our approach."

**Q: What's your security track record?**
A: "Security is foundational: 1) Smart contracts audited by [Trail of Bits/OpenZeppelin], 2) [X] bug bounty program, 3) Multi-sig treasury (4-of-7), 4) No security incidents to date. We budget $300K+ annually for ongoing security."

### 4.4 Team & Execution

**Q: Why is your team uniquely positioned?**
A: "[Founder 1] built [relevant experience], [Founder 2] scaled [relevant achievement], and our advisors include [notable names]. Combined, we have [X] years in crypto/fintech and have [specific relevant accomplishment]."

**Q: What keeps you up at night?**
A: "Honestly: 1) Regulatory uncertainty - we mitigate with compliance-first approach, 2) Competition from well-funded players - we focus on execution speed, 3) Market adoption timing - we're prepared for both bull and bear scenarios."

### 4.5 Investment & Returns

**Q: What's your expected valuation at exit?**
A: "Conservative scenario: $500M (5x return on Series A). Base case: $2B+ (15x+ return). The prediction market leaders (Polymarket at $9B, Kalshi at $11B) suggest significant upside potential."

**Q: What are the key milestones before Series B?**
A: "To raise Series B, we need: 1) 500K+ users, 2) $2B+ annual trading volume, 3) Clear path to $100M ARR, 4) At least one regulatory license. This round gives us 24 months runway to achieve these."

| Kalshi | $11B | 40x fee revenue |
| dYdX | ~$1B (FDV) | Protocol revenue based |

### 5.2 Valuation Approaches

**Revenue Multiple (For Revenue-Generating Companies):**
```
Valuation = Revenue × Multiple

Early-stage crypto: 10-15x revenue
Growth-stage crypto: 15-25x revenue
High-growth crypto: 25x+ revenue

Example:
- Year 1 ARR: $3.5M
- Multiple: 15x
- Valuation: $52.5M
```

**Volume-Based (For Pre-Revenue):**
```
Valuation = Trading Volume × Take Rate Potential × Multiple

Example:
- Monthly Volume: $50M
- Potential Take Rate: 0.5%
- Annualized Fee Revenue: $3M
- Multiple: 20x
- Implied Valuation: $60M
```

**Comparable Transaction:**
```
Recent Raises:
- Polymarket: $2B at $9B (Oct 2025)
- Kalshi: $1B at $11B (Nov 2025)
- dYdX: $65M at ~$500M (2021)

Median multiple for prediction markets:
~20-40x trailing volume or 100x+ fee revenue
```

### 5.3 Valuation Negotiation

**Pre-Money vs. Post-Money:**
```
Pre-Money Valuation: Value before investment
Post-Money Valuation: Value after investment

Example:
- Raising: $15M
- Pre-money: $60M
- Post-money: $75M
- Dilution: 20%
```

**Option Pool:**
- Standard: 10-15% for Series A
- Consider: Is pool created pre or post-money?
- Impact: Pre-money pool increases effective dilution

## 6. Term Sheet Key Terms

### 6.1 Economic Terms

**Valuation:**
- Pre-money valuation
- Post-money valuation
- Price per share

**Investment Amount:**
- Total round size
- Lead investor amount
- Pro-rata rights for existing investors

**Equity Structure:**
```
Standard Series A Structure:

Common Stock: Founders, employees
Preferred Stock: Investors

Preferred Rights:
- Liquidation preference (1x standard)
- Participation rights (participating vs non-participating)
- Anti-dilution protection (weighted average)
- Dividend rights (typically non-cumulative)
```

### 6.2 Control Terms

**Board Composition:**
```
Typical Series A Board:
- 2 Founders
- 1 Lead Investor
- 0-1 Independent (often added at Series B)
```

**Protective Provisions:**
- Sale/merger of company
- New equity issuance
- Debt above threshold
- Changes to charter
- Related party transactions

**Voting Rights:**
- Standard: 1 share = 1 vote
- Founder-friendly: Dual-class structure

### 6.3 Crypto-Specific Terms

**Token Provisions (SAFE + Token Warrant):**
```
Token Warrant Terms:
- Token allocation: Usually 15-25% of equity stake
- Pro-rata rights to future tokens
- Token purchase price: Discount (10-30%) to public price
- Lock-up: 1 year cliff, 2-3 year vesting
- Regulatory out: If token deemed illegal
```

**Key Token Considerations:**
- Equity-to-token ratio (e.g., 4:1)
- Token vesting schedule
- Lock-up periods
- Registration rights

### 6.4 Red Flags to Avoid

| Term | Red Flag | Acceptable |
|------|----------|------------|
| Liquidation Preference | > 1x participating | 1x non-participating |
| Anti-dilution | Full ratchet | Weighted average |
| Board Control | Investor majority | Balanced/founder-friendly |
| Redemption Rights | Mandatory | None or investor-request only |
| Founder Vesting | 4-year restart | Credit for time served |

## 8. Fundraising Timeline

### 8.1 Typical Series A Timeline

```
Week -4 to 0: Preparation
├── Finalize pitch deck
├── Update financial model
├── Prepare data room
├── Build investor target list
└── Get warm intros queued

Week 1-2: Launch
├── Send deck to 20-30 target investors
├── Schedule first meetings
├── Begin partner meetings
└── Iterate on pitch based on feedback

Week 3-4: First Meetings
├── 2-3 partner meetings per day
├── Second meetings with interested parties
├── Address common questions
└── Start technical diligence with leads

Week 5-6: Diligence
├── Deep dives with top 3-5 prospects
├── Reference checks
├── Technical/product diligence
├── Legal review

Week 7-8: Term Sheets
├── Receive term sheets
├── Negotiate terms
├── Select lead investor
└── Begin closing process

Week 9-12: Closing
├── Final legal documentation
├── Board approval
├── Wire transfer
└── Announce round
```

## 9. Due Diligence Preparation

### 9.1 Technical Diligence

**Code Review Preparation:**
- Clean, documented codebase
- README with setup instructions
- Architecture documentation
- Test coverage report (aim for >80%)
- Security audit reports readily available

**Common Technical Questions:**
1. Explain your smart contract architecture
2. How do you handle oracle failures?
3. What's your approach to scalability?
4. How do you secure user funds?
5. What's your incident response process?

### 9.2 Financial Diligence

**Prepare to Explain:**
- Revenue recognition policies
- Key assumptions in financial model
- Sensitivity analysis
- Working capital needs
- Currency/crypto exposure

**Documentation Needed:**
- Monthly P&L (last 12 months)
- Balance sheet
- Cash flow statement
- Bank reconciliations
- Revenue by source/customer

### 9.3 Legal Diligence

**Common Issues to Address:**
- Regulatory compliance status
- Intellectual property ownership
- Employment agreements (especially IP assignment)
- Cap table cleanliness
- Any past or pending litigation

### 9.4 Reference Check Preparation

**Prepare References:**
- 3+ customers/users (if applicable)
- 2+ partners/vendors
- Former colleagues of founders
- Industry experts
- Previous investors
