## Team Evolution by Stage

### Pre-Seed Stage (3-5 People)

At pre-seed, focus on versatile founders who can wear multiple hats. Every hire should be a force multiplier.

| Role | Responsibilities | Profile |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **CEO/Co-founder** | Vision, fundraising, business development, legal coordination | Serial entrepreneur or domain expert in prediction markets/DeFi |
| **CTO/Co-founder** | Architecture, smart contracts, initial product development | Full-stack + blockchain expertise, previous startup experience |
| **Lead Engineer** | Core platform development, DevOps, security | Senior full-stack with Solidity experience |
| **Product/Design** | UX research, product design, user testing | Generalist who can handle product and design |
| **Community/BD** | Community building, early partnerships, content | Crypto-native with strong network |

**Key Characteristics:**
- All equity, minimal cash compensation
- Everyone codes or directly contributes to product
- No managers - individual contributors only
- 80+ hour weeks expected during crunch

### Seed Stage (8-12 People)

Post-seed funding, build out core engineering and establish go-to-market functions.

| Function | Headcount | Roles |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Engineering** | 5-6 | CTO, 2 Smart Contract Engineers, 2 Full-Stack Engineers, 1 DevOps |
| **Product** | 2 | Product Manager, UI/UX Designer |
| **Growth** | 2-3 | Community Manager, Marketing Lead, BD Lead |
| **Operations** | 1-2 | Legal/Compliance (fractional), Operations Manager |

**Organizational Priorities:**
1. Ship v1 product to mainnet
2. Establish security practices and first audit
3. Build initial community (Discord, Twitter)
4. Regulatory clarity in target jurisdictions

### Series A Stage (20-30 People)

With Series A, scale engineering and establish proper team structures.

| Function | Headcount | Roles |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Engineering** | 10-12 | CTO, Engineering Manager, 4 Smart Contract Engineers, 4 Full-Stack Engineers, 2 DevOps/SRE |
| **Product** | 4 | Head of Product, 2 Product Managers, 2 UI/UX Designers |
| **Growth** | 6-8 | Head of Marketing, Content Lead, 2 Community Managers, Partnerships Lead, Growth Lead, PR Manager |
| **Operations** | 4-6 | COO, Legal Counsel (in-house), Compliance Manager, Finance Manager, HR/People Ops, Customer Support Lead |

**Organizational Priorities:**
1. Multi-chain expansion
2. Enterprise-grade reliability (99.9% uptime)
3. Regulatory licenses in key jurisdictions
4. International expansion

## Job Descriptions

### Smart Contract Engineer

**About the Role:**
We are seeking a Smart Contract Engineer to design, develop, and maintain the core protocol powering our prediction market platform. You will work on Solidity smart contracts handling millions in TVL, implement novel market mechanisms, and ensure the highest security standards.

**Responsibilities:**
- Design and implement smart contracts for prediction market mechanics (AMM, order books, resolution)
- Write comprehensive test suites achieving 100% code coverage
- Conduct internal security reviews and prepare for external audits
- Optimize gas efficiency for all contract interactions
- Document protocol specifications and integration guides
- Participate in incident response and on-call rotation
- Collaborate with frontend team on Web3 integration

**Requirements:**
- 3+ years of Solidity development experience
- Deep understanding of EVM, gas optimization, and common vulnerabilities
- Experience with testing frameworks (Hardhat, Foundry, Forge)
- Knowledge of DeFi primitives (AMMs, lending, oracles)
- Familiarity with formal verification tools (Certora, Halmos) is a plus
- Previous audit experience (either conducting or responding to audits)
- Strong communication skills for technical documentation

**Nice to Have:**
- Experience with prediction markets or derivatives protocols
- Contributions to open-source DeFi projects
- Published security research or bug bounty participation
- Experience with L2s (Optimism, Arbitrum, Base)
- Rust/Move experience for multi-chain expansion

## Remote vs Hybrid Considerations

### Remote-First Model (Recommended)

#### Advantages
- Access to global crypto talent pool
- Lower cost of living adjustments
- Asynchronous work across time zones
- Flexibility attracts top candidates

#### Requirements
- Minimum 4-hour overlap with core team hours
- Strong written communication skills
- Self-motivated and accountable
- Reliable internet and professional workspace

#### Supporting Infrastructure
- All meetings recorded and documented
- Comprehensive internal wiki (Notion)
- Async-first communication defaults
- Regular virtual social events

### Hybrid Model

#### When to Consider
- Legal/compliance roles needing in-person presence
- Early-stage (<10 people) for rapid iteration
- Specific jurisdiction requirements
- Team preference for in-person collaboration

#### Hub Locations to Consider
- **New York:** Regulatory proximity, finance talent
- **San Francisco:** Crypto VC network, engineering talent
- **Lisbon:** Crypto-friendly, lower cost, EU access
- **Singapore:** Asia-Pacific hub, clear regulation
- **Dubai:** Crypto-friendly jurisdiction, regional hub

### Time Zone Strategy

| Time Zone Clusters | Coverage | Team Composition |
|--------------------|----------|------------------|
| Americas (UTC-8 to UTC-4) | 9am-6pm PT | Primary engineering |
| Europe (UTC to UTC+2) | 9am-6pm CET | Ops, compliance, some engineering |
| Asia-Pacific (UTC+8 to UTC+12) | 9am-6pm SGT | Support, community, engineering |

**Recommendation:** Cluster teams by function to maximize overlap, with 2-3 hour "global overlap" windows for cross-team sync.
